#### Types of Space System Architectures
There are several types of space system architectures, each with its own unique characteristics and purposes. These include:

- Monolithic Architecture: This type of architecture involves a single, large spacecraft that performs all functions and operations. It is typically used for missions that require a high level of reliability and redundancy.
- Distributed Architecture: In this type of architecture, multiple smaller spacecraft work together to perform a specific task or function. This allows for greater flexibility and adaptability in mission design.
- Cluster Architecture: Similar to distributed architecture, cluster architecture involves multiple spacecraft working together, but in a more coordinated and synchronized manner. This is often used for missions that require precise measurements or observations.
- Network Architecture: This type of architecture involves a network of interconnected spacecraft that work together to achieve a common goal. It is often used for communication and navigation missions.

#### Classes of Space System Architectures
In addition to types, space system architectures can also be classified into different classes based on their complexity and capabilities. These include:

- Simple Systems: These are basic space systems that perform a single function or task, such as communication or Earth observation.
- Complex Systems: These are more advanced space systems that involve multiple components and perform a variety of functions, such as remote sensing or space exploration.
- Interconnected Systems: These are space systems that are composed of multiple interconnected components, such as the International Space Station or the Global Positioning System.

Overall, understanding the different types and classes of space system architectures is crucial in designing and operating successful space missions. It allows for better decision-making and optimization of resources, ultimately leading to more efficient and effective space systems.

The monolithic architecture also allowed for easier communication and control of the rover from Earth.

#### Case Study 2: Distributed Architecture in the International Space Station
The International Space Station (ISS) is a prime example of a distributed architecture in space systems. The ISS is a large, habitable artificial satellite that orbits Earth and serves as a research laboratory for various scientific experiments.

The ISS is composed of multiple modules, each with its own specific function, such as living quarters, laboratories, and storage areas. These modules are connected by trusses and solar arrays, creating a distributed architecture that allows for flexibility and adaptability. This architecture also allows for the addition of new modules and upgrades to existing ones, making the ISS a continuously evolving space system.

The Hubble Space Telescope (HST) is a prime example of a simple architecture in space systems. The HST is a large space telescope that was launched in 1990 and has been in operation ever since.

The HST's architecture is relatively simple, with a single primary mirror and a few supporting instruments. This simplicity allows for easier maintenance and repair missions, as well as cost-effectiveness in the initial design and construction of the telescope. However, the simple architecture also limits the capabilities of the HST, as it cannot be upgraded or expanded like the ISS.

This complex architecture allowed for a wide range of scientific observations and experiments to be conducted, making it one of the most successful space missions to date.
