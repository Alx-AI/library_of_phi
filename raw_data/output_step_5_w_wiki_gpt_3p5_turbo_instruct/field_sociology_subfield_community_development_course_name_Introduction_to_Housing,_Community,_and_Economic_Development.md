#### 9.1a Examining the role of local governance in community development

Local governance refers to the systems and processes through which decisions are made and implemented at the local level. This includes decisions made by local governments, community organizations, and other stakeholders within a community. The concept of local governance is based on the idea that decisions made at the local level are more effective and sustainable, as they are tailored to the specific needs and context of a community.

One of the key roles of local governance in community development is promoting community participation and partnership. This means involving community members in decision-making processes and empowering them to take an active role in shaping their own development. This approach is in contrast to earlier top-down approaches, where decisions were made by state agendas and resource control.

Local governance also involves decentralization of government power to local communities. This means shifting decision-making power away from the state and/or national governments to the grassroots level. This allows for more inclusive and participatory decision-making processes, as local communities have a better understanding of their own needs and priorities.

However, local governance also faces challenges in promoting community development. One of the main challenges is ensuring the legitimacy of decisions made at the local level. This depends on the participation rate of the local population and how well the participants represent the community. Without proper representation and participation, decisions may not accurately reflect the needs and priorities of the community.

Another challenge is the interplay between local governance and governance at other scales. Decisions made at the national or global level can have a significant impact on local communities, and vice versa. For example, national policies on housing or economic development can greatly affect the development of a local community. Therefore, it is important for local governance to consider and engage with decisions made at other scales.

In conclusion, local governance plays a crucial role in community development by promoting community participation and decentralization of power. However, it also faces challenges in ensuring the legitimacy of decisions and navigating the interplay between governance at different scales. Understanding these complexities is essential for promoting sustainable and equitable development at the local level.

#### Types of Housing Markets

There are two main types of housing markets: rental and ownership. Rental markets involve the leasing of housing units to tenants, while ownership markets involve the buying and selling of homes. Both types of markets are influenced by supply and demand, as well as various economic and social factors.

Rental markets are typically more volatile than ownership markets, as they are more sensitive to changes in the economy and housing policies. In rental markets, landlords set the rent prices based on the demand for housing and the cost of maintaining the property. In contrast, ownership markets are influenced by factors such as mortgage interest rates, housing prices, and income levels.

#### Dynamics of Housing Markets

Housing markets are characterized by boom and bust cycles, where housing prices experience periods of rapid growth followed by declines. According to economists, housing price increases beyond the general inflation rate are not sustainable in the long term. This was evident in the subprime mortgage crisis of 2008, where the housing bubble burst and led to a global financial crisis.

The housing bubble was fueled by low interest rates and easy credit conditions, which allowed for higher rates of household debt and lower savings rates. This resulted in a slight increase in home ownership rates and a significant increase in housing prices. The bubble was more pronounced in coastal areas where the ability to build new housing was restricted by geography or land use restrictions.

#### Impact on Communities

The dynamics of housing markets have a significant impact on communities. Rapidly rising housing prices can lead to gentrification, where low-income residents are displaced by wealthier individuals. This can result in the loss of community cohesion and cultural diversity. On the other hand, declining housing prices can lead to foreclosures and abandoned properties, which can negatively impact the overall appearance and safety of a community.

Moreover, housing markets also play a crucial role in the economic development of a community. Affordable housing is essential for attracting and retaining a skilled workforce, which is necessary for economic growth. Housing policies that promote affordable housing can also stimulate economic development by creating jobs and increasing consumer spending.

#### Energy Efficiency Policies and Regulations

One of the key areas of focus in housing policies and regulations is energy efficiency. With the increasing concern for climate change and the need to reduce carbon emissions, many countries have implemented regulations to promote energy-efficient building construction. For instance, in the United Kingdom, the 2003 Energy White Paper set a target to cut overall emissions by 60% by 2050 and by 80% by 2100. As a result, in 2006, the Building Regulations were tightened to require a 20% reduction in energy use in new housing compared to the 2002 standards.

To achieve this, the primary measure of energy efficiency was changed from u-value to the Dwelling Carbon Dioxide Emission Rate (DER). This rate takes into account various factors such as airtightness, heating and lighting efficiency, and the use of renewable energy sources. Additionally, it became compulsory to upgrade the energy efficiency of existing houses when certain works were carried out. However, there have been criticisms regarding the effectiveness of these policies in achieving the 20% reduction target.

#### Economic Policies and Regulations

Economic policies and regulations also play a significant role in shaping the housing market. Government policies also impact the housing market through regulations on lending practices and subsidies for affordable housing. For example, in the United States, the Community Reinvestment Act (CRA) was enacted to encourage banks to lend to low and moderate-income individuals and communities. ### Section: 14.1 Land trusts and their role in housing

Land trusts have gained popularity in recent years as an alternative housing model that prioritizes community ownership and control of housing. In this section, we will explore the concept of land trusts and their impact on affordable housing.

#### Understanding the concept of land trusts and their impact on affordable housing

A land trust is a non-profit organization that acquires and holds land for the purpose of creating affordable housing. The concept of land trusts has historic roots in indigenous Americas, pre-colonial Africa, and ancient Chinese economic systems. The goal of land trusts is to restore the concept of community ownership and control of land, rather than initiating it.

Land trusts can take various forms, such as community land trusts, real estate investment trusts, and conservation land trusts. Community land trusts trace their conceptual history to England's Garden Cities, India's Gramdan Movement, and Israel's cooperative agricultural settlements. These trusts prioritize community involvement in decision-making processes and aim to create long-term affordability.
